# Brandivibe — Competitive Battlecards

A battlecard for every likely alternative. The AI sales brain reads these when drafting responses to objections.

---

## Battlecard #1 — basement.studio

**Type**: Tier 1 direct competitor (premium independent studio)

**Overview**
- HQ: Argentina (remote team)
- Team: ~10 people
- Founded: 2020
- Pricing: ~$80-200K per project
- Notable clients: Vercel, Ethereum Foundation, Readymag

**What they say**
- *"We make cool shit that performs."*
- Oversized editorial typography, scroll-choreographed storytelling

**Reality**
- They're a team, not one person — so they charge for overhead
- 12-16 week typical timeline
- Excellent editorial/typographic taste, lean Framer Motion-heavy
- Less R3F depth than us (they prefer CSS + Motion)

**Our advantages vs. basement.studio**
1. **~50% cheaper** — $35-90K vs. their $80-200K
2. **~50% faster** — 6 weeks vs. their 12-16
3. **More 3D depth** — Our heroes run live R3F with custom shaders; they lean on CSS animation + Framer Motion
4. **Direct founder access** — You talk to the person building it
5. **Public GitHub** — We ship an open repo; they deliver private

**When to win**
- Budget ceiling at $50-80K
- Needs launch in <8 weeks
- Wants real 3D, not just typographic motion
- Solo founder who hates account managers

**When we lose**
- Enterprise client with $200K budget (they'll take basement for the brand safety)
- Team-based buying committee (basement team feels less risky)
- Editorial-only project with no 3D (basement is stronger there)

**Talk track**
> "I love basement — they were one of my reference studios when I started. Honest framing: if you have $150K+ and 14 weeks, book them. If you have $40-80K and 6 weeks, I can give you 90% of the Basement-tier craft with more 3D depth. Here's how I'd position your project vs. their Vercel site..."

## Battlecard #2 — studio freight

**Type**: Tier 1 direct competitor (premium editorial studio)

**Overview**
- HQ: Colombia
- Team: ~15 people
- Notable for: Lenis (smooth scroll library, which we use), Hamo (React hooks library)
- Pricing: ~$100-250K

**What they say**
- Restrained minimalism + smooth-scroll perfect

**Reality**
- Incredible taste — probably the tastiest independent studio alive
- Open-sourced their core libraries (which we use, so we don't have to reinvent)
- 12-16 weeks typical
- Heavier on motion polish, lighter on 3D spectacle

**Our advantages**
1. **Faster (6 wks)** and **cheaper ($35-90K)**
2. **Broader aesthetic range** — 8 demos across editorial → cinematic → minimal. Studio Freight is minimal-only.
3. **More R3F depth** — They're polished but lean. We go deeper in WebGL.

**When to win**
- Crypto/gaming/luxury project that wants aggressive 3D — not their comfort zone
- Client who can't wait 14 weeks

**When we lose**
- Client who specifically wants the "studio freight minimalism" look — we can imitate but they're the original
- $200K+ budgets that want the Lenis creators themselves

**Talk track**
> "Funny story — we use Lenis on every Brandivibe project. Studio Freight open-sourced it. So in a way we all use their tech. Difference is they're a 15-person agency focused on editorial minimalism and we're a one-person studio with broader aesthetic range. If you're picking between the two: studio freight for pure editorial, us if you want 3D ambition."

## Battlecard #3 — Webflow template / Framer template + tweaks

**Type**: Tier 2 "good enough" alternative (the 95% case we actually compete against)

**Overview**
- DIY or hire Upwork freelancer for $2-10K
- Output: one of 20 popular templates, lightly customized

**Reality**
- Solves the "we have a website" problem, not the "our site is differentiating" problem
- Every YC batch looks identical on Webflow
- Fine for pre-revenue. Embarrassing post-Series A.

**Our advantages**
1. **Differentiation** — You stop looking like every other YC batch 12
2. **Code ownership** — You're not locked into Webflow's platform
3. **Performance** — Custom Next.js outperforms Webflow on Lighthouse
4. **Scale** — Webflow templates break at 100+ pages, custom site doesn't
5. **Taste signal** — Investors, recruits, press, Twitter all recognize template sites

**When to win**
- Client past seed who can't use "we're early" as an excuse anymore
- Founder who actually cares about brand (see ICP psychographics)
- Company with a real launch driving urgency

**When we lose**
- True pre-revenue founder who should honestly just use Webflow
- Budget truly capped under $10K
- Client doesn't yet believe brand matters (losing to inaction)

**Talk track**
> "A Webflow template is the right call for a 5-person pre-seed. You're past that. Every Series A-announced company this week has the same Webflow template and you're competing with them for talent, funds, and press attention. Your founders didn't raise $8M to look like 200 other companies. Brand is compression — we give you 6 weeks of it."

## Battlecard #4 — Hire an in-house designer

**Type**: Tier 3 alternative (the "build" vs "buy" framing)

**Overview**
- Fully loaded cost of a senior designer: $180-220K/year (US), $120-160K (EU)
- Time to hire: 2-4 months average
- Time to first shipped site: 4-6 months from day one (hire + onboard + figma + build)

**Reality**
- Great for companies at Series B+ with consistent design needs
- Terrible ROI for a single site project
- You'll spend $80K in salary + equity + benefits before the first site launches

**Our advantages**
1. **4-5x faster to first launched site** — 6 weeks vs. 6 months
2. **Cheaper for single-project scope** — $50K vs. $90K+ (6 mo of fully loaded designer salary)
3. **Specialist skills** — You hire a generalist; we're specialists in R3F + premium brand sites
4. **No onboarding tax** — We already know our toolkit deeply

**When to win**
- Single-project scope (one brand site)
- Urgency (event/launch in <3 months)
- Brand is NOT their ongoing discipline (they just need one great site)

**When we lose**
- Company needs FT brand work beyond the site
- Already started the hiring process
- Wants brand + product design + marketing + social + illustration — bigger than our scope

**Talk track**
> "You should hire a senior designer if you're going to need brand work every week for the next 18 months. If you need ONE outstanding site in the next 6 weeks, that hiring process takes 3 months before they write a line of code. We start Monday. You can still hire later — this frees that hire to focus on product instead of marketing site."

## Battlecard #5 — Do nothing (status quo)

**Type**: Tier 4 — the REAL competitor. 70% of lost deals.

**What they say**
- *"We'll do it after the next round"*
- *"Not a priority this quarter"*
- *"Our site is fine for now"*

**Reality**
- "After the next round" means "12 more months of looking generic to every prospect/investor/recruit"
- The site IS the prospecting surface. Fixing it is leverage on everything else
- Most founders don't realize until AFTER a bad press cycle or failed recruiting week

**How to win against inaction**
1. **Urgency trigger** — Find a specific date (launch, TGE, event, round close) and anchor the quote to "site live by [date]"
2. **Math the cost of waiting** — "12 more months of looking generic = X lost recruits × $Y cost per role = more than the site"
3. **Social proof with FOMO** — "Your direct competitor [X] just rebranded with [studio]. You're next either way."
4. **Frame it as compression, not expense** — "We compress 6 months of brand + design + build into 6 weeks. That's what you're buying — time."
5. **De-risk** — "Fixed price, fixed scope, public repo, 50% on milestone 1, you can cancel before week 2 with 0% penalty."

**Talk track against inaction**
> "I'd rather you decide 'no' today than decide 'later' — because 'later' costs you 12 months of looking like every other [vertical] while competitors catch up. Here's my ask: pick one of these 8 demos that's closest to your brand, tell me you hate it or love it, and I'll send a fixed-scope proposal in 48 hours. If it's not compelling, ignore it. If it is, we start Monday."

## How the AI brain uses these battlecards

When a prospect replies to a cold email with an objection, the AI brain:
1. Classifies the objection → matches to one battlecard
2. Pulls the relevant `Talk track`
3. Personalizes with prospect's name/company/specific context
4. Drafts the response for human review
5. Tracks outcome: did this battlecard win or lose the deal?

Over time the AI learns which talk tracks work for which objection patterns and improves them.
